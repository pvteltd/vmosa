GLOBAL CHARTER OF THE NATURALIST

Preamble:


We, the Naturalists, acknowledging the divine guidance provided in the Quran and recognizing the importance of safeguarding minority and natural rights, do hereby establish this Global Charter for the Naturalists to preserve and promote the values and principles enshrined within.

Article I: Fundamental Principles

Section 1: The Negation (لا إله)

To safeguard and promote minority and natural rights, ensuring that the power of civil disobedience is protected and maintained for the benefit of all.

Section 2: The Affirmation (إلا الله)

The natural liberty of humanity is to be free from any superior power on Earth and not to be under the will or legislative authority of man. The preservation of natural liberty as a fundamental right of all individuals, as provided in verses 60:8-9.

Section 3: Muhammad as the Messenger of Natural Rights (محمد رسول الله)

Recognizing the traits of Muhammad, as described in the Quran, as a symbol of advocacy for natural rights and as guidance for evaluating leadership qualifications based on the highest expectation of fulfilling the condition precedent لا إله.

a) Unity and Oneness:

Understanding that the Law of Nature, Nature's God, prophets, rasuls, believers of natural law, or the naturalists are considered "One Nation"; regardless of their beliefs, and that allegiance to these principles is, in fact, allegiance to the unity of a just and harmonious world. This unity One God/Good is essential for fostering an inclusive approach to the pursuit of natural rights, justice, and harmony, embracing individuals from diverse backgrounds and beliefs.

b) No Distinction:

Acknowledging that there should be no distinction made between the teachings of various prophets, rasuls, believers of natural law, or the naturalists in the context of natural rights. This understanding fosters an inclusive approach to the pursuit of One God/Good, natural rights, justice, and harmony, embracing individuals from diverse backgrounds and beliefs.

c) Avoiding Division:

Recognizing the importance of avoiding divisions among those who uphold the principles of natural rights and those who reject them. The true goal is to strive for unity and harmony in upholding the principles of natural law and promoting One God/Good or natural rights for all.

d) Common Ground and Collaboration:

Inviting people of different belief systems and legal traditions to find common ground in the shared pursuit of One God/Good, natural rights, justice, and harmony. This approach encourages collaboration and mutual understanding, fostering a more inclusive and respectful ecological justice for all.

e) Implementing the Principles:

Emphasizing the importance of implementing the principles of natural rights, as described in the Quranic teachings; Surah 103 and verses 30:30, 11:17 and 12:108, in order to establish a strong foundation for a just and harmonious world. By adhering to these principles, individuals and societies can work together to create a just and harmonious world, free from the divisions and conflicts that arise from rejecting the common values of natural law.

Article II: Major Natural Rights

Section 1: Right to Life and Dignity

Every individual shall have the right to life and dignity, and no one shall be deprived of this right arbitrarily.

Section 2: Right to Freedom of Belief and Expression

Every individual shall have the right to freedom of belief, thought, and expression, subject to the respect for the natural rights and freedoms of others. This right shall be founded on the principles of "do no harm" and "do not let others harm you."

Section 3: Right to Justice and Equality

The people and its laws shall refrain from employing force or coercion unless there is evidence of harm to others.

Section 4: Right to Property and Resources

Every individual shall have the right to own, inherit, and manage property and resources in accordance with the principles of justice and equity.

Section 5: Right to Family and Community

Every individual shall have the right to establish a family and participate in the life of their community, contributing to the well-being and development of society.

Article III: Implementation and Preservation

Section 1: Upholding the Naturalist Constitution

Individuals, communities, and nations shall strive to integrate the Quranic principles of 60:8-9 into their legal and political systems, in order to safeguard minority and natural rights.

Section 2: Promoting Natural Rights

Individuals shall take responsibility for promoting and preserving natural rights in their communities, guided by the Quranic principles enshrined in this Global Charter.

Article IV: Non-Amendability

The provisions of this Global Charter for the Naturalists shall not be amended, ensuring that the principles and values enshrined within remain steadfast and unwavering in the pursuit of a just and equitable society.

Article V: Environmental Stewardship and Sustainability

Section 1: Protection of Natural Environment

Individuals, communities, and nations shall strive to preserve and protect the natural environment and its resources for present and future generations, guided by the principles of equitable balance and sustainability.

Section 2: Sustainable Development

Development shall be pursued according to the Principle of Gift Economics, and in a manner that is sustainable, equitable, and respects the delicate balance of ecosystems, ensuring the well-being and prosperity of all living beings.

Section 3: Conservation and Restoration

Efforts shall be made to conserve biodiversity, rehabilitate degraded ecosystems, and restore the balance of nature, thereby contributing to the overall health and harmony of our planet.

Article VI: Education and Enlightenment

Section 1: Right to Education

Every individual shall have the right to education that promotes the understanding and appreciation of natural rights, Quranic principles, and the interconnectedness of all living beings.

Section 2: Promotion of Knowledge and Wisdom

Individuals, communities, and nations shall strive to acquire and disseminate knowledge and wisdom, fostering a culture of enlightenment and critical thinking for the betterment of society.

Section 3: Nurturing Ethical Values

Education shall promote ethical values and principles, nurturing responsible and compassionate citizens who actively contribute to the well-being of their communities and the world at large.

Article VII: International Cooperation and Solidarity

Section 1: Promotion of Peace and Harmony

Individuals, communities, and nations shall work together to promote peace and harmony, resolving conflicts through dialogue and mutual understanding, and striving to uphold the principles enshrined in this Global Charter.

Section 2: Collaboration for Sustainable Development

Partnerships shall be established across borders to address global challenges, such as poverty, inequality, and environmental degradation, fostering a spirit of gift economy, international cooperation and solidarity.

Section 3: Respect for Diversity and Sovereignty

The sovereignty and cultural diversity of nations shall be respected and upheld, with the understanding that the principles enshrined in this Global Charter for the Naturalists serve as a common foundation for the preservation of natural rights and the betterment of humankind.

Article VIII: Enforcement and Accountability

Section 1: Upholding the Principles of the Charter

Individuals, communities, and nations shall be responsible for upholding and adhering to the principles set forth in this Global Charter for the Naturalists, ensuring the preservation of natural rights for all.

Section 2: Monitoring and Reporting

A system of monitoring and reporting shall be established to assess progress towards the realization of the principles and objectives outlined in this Charter, with the aim of fostering transparency and accountability.

Section 3: Addressing Violations

Appropriate measures shall be taken to address violations of the principles enshrined in this Charter, including but not limited to diplomatic, legal, and economic means, always prioritizing peaceful resolution and the protection of natural rights.

Article IX: Interpretation and Guidance

Section 1: Interpretation of the Charter

This Global Charter for the Naturalists shall be interpreted in good faith and in accordance with the principles of natural law, Quranic teachings, and the broader objectives of promoting natural rights, justice, and harmony.

Section 2: Metaphysical Interpretation Methodology

a) The Study Guide Surahs 96, 68, 73, 74, and 1 shall be interpreted through a metaphysical lens, ensuring that their teachings align with the principles of unity and understanding, as laid out in verses 75:19, 11:17, 3:64, 4:82, and Surah 103.

b) Interpretation of the Study Guide Surahs shall prioritize their underlying messages and themes, transcending religious and cultural differences, fostering inclusivity and mutual respect.

c) The metaphysical interpretation methodology shall be guided by a focus on spiritual growth, ethical living, preservation of freewill and freedom, and the promotion of the common good, recognizing the interconnectedness of all living beings.

Section 3: Role of Spiritual and Intellectual Leaders

Spiritual and intellectual leaders from diverse backgrounds shall play a vital role in guiding the metaphysical interpretation and application of the principles set forth in this Charter, fostering an environment of open dialogue, critical inquiry, and mutual respect.

Section 4: Continual Reassessment and Adaptation

As the world evolves and new challenges emerge, the metaphysical, epistemological and axiological elemens of the metaphysical interpretation methodology shall be subject to continual reassessment and adaptation to concrete scientific facts, ensuring its ongoing relevance and effectiveness in promoting the preservation of natural rights and the naturalistic way of life.

Section 5: Collaboration and Cooperation

Individuals, communities, and nations are encouraged to collaborate and cooperate in the development and implementation of the metaphysical interpretation methodology, recognizing the importance of unity and understanding in achieving a world where justice, harmony, and well-being prevail for all living beings.

Signatories:

In Witness Whereof, the undersigned, duly authorized representatives, have affixed their signatures to this Global Charter for the Naturalists, committing to uphold and promote the principles and values enshrined herein, for the benefit of all living beings and the natural world.
